## Executive Summary Report: 
Healthcare Revenue & Patient Demographics
This report outlines key financial findings, patient demographic trends, actionable insights, and strategic recommendations to optimize hospital revenue and resource allocation. All financial figures are reported in Nigerian Naira (₦).

## 1. Key Findings & Financial Observations
### Revenue Trends & Seasonality
 * *Annual Revenue Collapse:* Total annual revenue remained stable and peaked between 2020 and 2023 (hovering around ₦280M annually). However, 2024 saw a *massive, sharp decline*, dropping well below ₦100M.
 * *Monthly Revenue Volatility:* Monthly revenue fluctuated between ₦105M and ₦122M. A severe dip is consistently observed in *February* (falling to roughly ₦106M), while peak revenues occur during the summer months (*June–August) and **October*.
### Revenue Drivers (Medication & Conditions)
 * *Top Earners:* *Aspirin* is the highest revenue-generating medication (₦283.7M), followed closely by Paracetamol (₦281.1M) and Lipitor (₦279.6M).
 * *Medical Conditions:* *Diabetes* brings in the highest revenue (₦236.4M), followed closely by Obesity (₦236.0M) and Arthritis (₦235.1M). Cancer brings in the lowest comparative revenue at ₦229.8M.
 * *Admission Types:* *Elective admissions* generate both the highest volume (18,473 patients) and the highest revenue (₦473.1M), followed closely by Urgent admissions. Emergency admissions trail in both categories.
### Insurance & Billing
 * *Top Insurance Providers:* *Cigna* leads with the highest billing amount (₦284.3M), followed closely by Medicare (₦282.9M). Aetna has the lowest billing amount among the top providers at ₦276.4M.
## 2. Patient Demographics & Operations
 * *Gender & Blood Type Stability:* The patient base is perfectly balanced, splits nearly 50/50 between Male (27,496) and Female (27,470). The three most common blood types are almost equally distributed: *A-* (6,898), *A+* (6,896), and *B+* (6,885).
 * *Age Distribution:* The highest hospital visitation comes from *Adults* (16,308) and the *Elderly* (16,033), followed by the Old demographic (12,048). Teenagers (1,677) represent the smallest patient volume.
 * *Prescription Habits:* *Lipitor* is the most prescribed medication (11,038 prescriptions), matching its position as a high-revenue driver, followed tightly by Ibuprofen (11,023).
## 3. Strategic Insights
> *The 2024 Revenue Crisis:* The drop in 2024 revenue requires immediate investigation. It indicates a systemic issue—such as data underreporting, major insurance contract losses, a severe drop in patient retention, or operational shutdowns.
> *High-Volume, Even Distribution:* The data shows remarkably uniform distributions across medications, blood types, and gender. This suggests a highly stable, predictable patient profile, meaning operational costs should be easy to forecast under normal circumstances.
> 
## 4. Recommendations
 * *Investigate the 2024 Revenue Drop:* Conduct an immediate operational and financial audit to determine why 2024 revenue plummeted. Verify if data for 2024 is incomplete or if there was a genuine loss in patient volume.
 * *Optimize Inventory for Chronic Conditions:* Since Diabetes, Obesity, and Arthritis are the primary revenue drivers, and Lipitor/Ibuprofen are the most prescribed drugs, ensure seamless supply chain management for these chronic disease treatments to avoid stockouts.
 * *Targeted Marketing & Services for Adults/Elderly:* Since Adults and the Elderly make up the vast majority of hospital visits, tailor wellness programs, geriatric care, and specialized clinics specifically to these age groups to maximize patient retention.
 * *Manage February Slack:* Implement promotional healthcare packages, elective procedure scheduling incentives, or routine check-up campaigns specifically in January/February to counteract the predictable seasonal dip in February revenue.
 * *Strengthen Insurance Partnerships:* Given that Cigna and Medicare drive the highest billing amounts, optimize claims processing workflows with these providers to ensure fast, hassle-free reimbursements.
